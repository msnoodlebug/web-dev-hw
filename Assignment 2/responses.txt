1.) Data Travels in packets. They can be put into a single
packet of information or if its a large file it will be split
up into smaller pieces and sent through different channels until
all the files arrive at the destination so that the file can be reassembles and looked at.
2.) An IP Address is the address of your computer or your devise and the
DNS is the server of the information you are going to. They both have
 addresses and exchange addresses so that you can find the website or object
  you are looking for faster.
3.) A cookie is information that is saves so that way the website
 can recognize who you are when you log back into it so you don't
  have to log onto the web sight every time you use it.
4.) There is the protocol identifier in the beginning of the line
 to identify what protocols to use. another part is the specific IP address
  of the sight or computer you are trying to get to.and then there is the resource name,
  the name of the picture, website ect.
